# Word Odyssey: Child Test Plan

## Authority of this document

This document is authoritative for the first observational test: protocol, observer behavior, evidence, and cohort thresholds. It tests [First Playable Experience](first_playable_experience.md) and informs [Prototype Decisions](prototype_decisions.md).

## Test purpose

Determine whether an approximately 12-year-old English-dominant learner at roughly Chinese elementary-school Grade 3 proficiency can understand and enjoy a Simplified Chinese Prototype 1:

> Explore Chinese → open another region → choose Chinese anchors → see a Chinese story light the forest → tap to explore → resume → direct the next beat

Also determine whether pinyin and English work as scaffolding without replacing attention to Chinese characters.

This is a product test, not a test of the learner’s intelligence or school performance.

## First cohort

Recommended first cohort: **5 learners**, tested individually, who are reasonably close to the owner-decided audience:

- approximately age 12;
- English native/stronger, near U.S. Grade 7 reading level;
- Chinese near elementary-school Grade 3 proficiency;
- able to work with Simplified Chinese, even if another writing system is also familiar;
- comfortable using an iPad in landscape orientation.

Record:

- age;
- home and school languages;
- years and type of Chinese study;
- Chinese writing system learned or preferred;
- approximate Chinese character recognition and reading fluency;
- pinyin familiarity;
- comfort reading short Chinese narratives;
- iPad familiarity;
- prior exposure to Word Odyssey.

## Adult setup

Say only:

> “This is a Chinese word adventure we are making. Explore it however you want. English and pinyin help are available if you need them. I’ll mostly watch because we want to learn what is clear or confusing. You can stop anytime.”

### The adult may explain

- there is no correct starting word or story choice;
- the learner may stop at any time;
- how to recover from a prototype defect;
- a Chinese pronunciation or meaning only after the learner has tried the available support and remains blocked.

### The adult should not explain initially

- what Current, Available, Visited, or Seen mean;
- which relationship to choose;
- that a relationship resets;
- whether a word is a 近义词 or 反义词 candidate;
- how to open a second region;
- which anchors to choose;
- that tapping a lit story word pauses;
- which story direction is best;
- when to use pinyin or English help.

If the learner is inactive for 10 seconds, ask a neutral question such as “What do you think you can do here?” At 20 seconds, offer the least specific hint. Record every hint and time.

## 10–15 minute observational script

### Stage 1 — Chinese starting choice (0:00–1:00)

Present Chinese starting words and story themes from the first playable scenario.

Record:

- time to first choice;
- whether characters, pinyin, English, or imagery drives the choice;
- whether the learner asks which is correct;
- whether the mystery themes feel age-appropriate;
- emotional response.

Success signal: chooses within 30 seconds and can say what the selected Chinese term means, with support if needed.

### Stage 2 — First Chinese semantic move (1:00–2:30)

Allow selection of 近义词 Similar and an Available destination.

Record:

- whether the Chinese relationship label or English subtitle is used;
- whether the learner understands that candidates are similar but not always interchangeable;
- whether the learner notices Available nodes;
- which text layer is read first;
- whether the new Current is identified;
- whether reset causes confusion;
- taps on inactive nodes.

Ask only if necessary:

> “Where are you now?”

### Stage 3 — State comprehension probe (2:30–3:30)

After at least two moves, show Current, Available, one Visited inactive node, and one Seen inactive node.

Use non-leading prompts:

- “Which Chinese word are you standing on?”
- “Which words could you go to now?”
- “Which word did you visit before?”
- “Which word did you see but not visit?”

Record accuracy, hesitation, and whether the learner relies on color, size, route, characters, pinyin, or English.

Do not teach the state labels.

### Stage 4 — Reciprocal relation and Map View (3:30–5:00)

Expose a reciprocal 近义词 move. Observe whether the former Current becomes understandable as a return only after 近义词 is reselected.

Open Map View with one Visited and one Seen node.

Record:

- recognition of the actual Chinese route;
- return through a Visited node;
- attempts to enter Seen directly;
- response to the bilingual Seen preview;
- whether Map View feels like orientation or another exercise;
- whether reduced pinyin/English still leaves the map recognizable.

### Stage 5 — Open and explore the 地图 region (approximately 4:00–6:00)

Allow the learner to choose a second-region portal and use 相关词 and 常用搭配.

Record:

- whether portals are noticed without prompting;
- whether the learner understands the new region is separate;
- whether 地图 → 线索 is accepted as Related rather than a synonym;
- whether phrase nodes such as 神秘线索 feel like meaningful destinations;
- whether English support is requested before or after considering the Chinese;
- any disputed or confusing relationship example.

If portals are ignored for 30 seconds, ask, “Do you see another place you might explore?”

### Stage 6 — Select Chinese anchors and character (approximately 5:00–7:00)

Offer Start Story, two anchor selections, and a preset avatar/nickname.

Record:

- whether the learner understands that the selected Chinese terms will shape the story;
- whether choices are deliberate;
- whether anchors come from different regions;
- whether one can be removed or replaced;
- reaction to the age-neutral avatar style;
- whether English labels help confirmation without dominating it.

Ask:

> “What do you expect to appear in the story?”

### Stage 7 — Chinese story lighting and scaffolding (approximately 6:00–8:00)

Play a beat containing anchors, one reused Chinese word, and one or two new Chinese words.

Before the learner opens full English help, record:

- whether the gist is understood from Chinese, context, and pinyin;
- gaze shifts between sentence and forest;
- recognition of anchor words;
- ability to match a lit node to the Chinese sentence;
- whether Narrative lines are mistaken for semantic relationships;
- which support is requested first: pinyin, word gloss, or sentence translation;
- whether the content feels intriguing and age-appropriate.

After support is used, ask:

> “Which Chinese words did the story light up, and what did they mean here?”

### Stage 8 — Tap 低语 to auto-pause (approximately 7:00–9:00)

Do not explain auto-pause. Observe whether the learner taps 低语 or another lit term.

Record:

- intentional versus accidental tap;
- understanding of the immediate pause;
- recognition of Adventure View;
- use of pinyin/English for the new word;
- whether 低语 → 耳语 is understood as a similar-meaning move;
- whether Resume remains discoverable.

If no tap occurs within 20 seconds, ask, “Is there a Chinese word in the story you want to explore?”

### Stage 9 — Resume and direct (approximately 8:00–11:00)

Allow Resume, contextual reuse of 耳语, and selection of 发光的罗盘 or another director choice.

Record:

- recognition that the story continued rather than restarted;
- recognition of the newly explored Chinese word in context;
- deliberate selection of a Chinese director option;
- whether the next beat reflects it;
- Chinese read aloud or paraphrased voluntarily;
- laughter, prediction, correction, or spontaneous story proposal.

### Stage 10 — Close (final 1–2 minutes)

Offer Continue, View Map, or Finish for now. Ask:

- “What did you think you were doing?”
- “Which Chinese word or phrase do you remember?”
- “When did you use English or pinyin help?”
- “Did any part feel too easy, too hard, or too young?”
- “What should happen next?”

## Focused scaffold probes

Use these only after ordinary observation so they do not alter first-use behavior.

### Character versus pinyin attention

Show one recently Visited node with pinyin temporarily reduced. Ask the learner to identify it. Restore pinyin immediately if needed. Record whether recognition came from characters, position, or memory.

### English gloss dependence

Show one familiar Available node with its English gloss hidden. Observe whether the learner considers the Chinese/pinyin first or immediately requests English. This is not scored as right or wrong.

### Story comprehension ladder

For one short sentence, record comprehension after each layer:

1. Chinese characters and visual context;
2. pinyin;
3. individual English glosses;
4. full English translation.

The goal is to discover the least intrusive scaffold that restores meaningful comprehension.

## First-cohort thresholds

Apply these to the recommended cohort of five.

### Success threshold

Proceed with the core loop when:

- at least 4 of 5 complete a first Chinese semantic move with no more than a neutral prompt;
- at least 4 of 5 identify Current and Available;
- at least 3 of 5 distinguish Visited from Seen without being taught the labels;
- at least 4 of 5 correctly explain the selected relationship in English or by example;
- at least 4 of 5 open a second region and select Chinese anchors deliberately;
- at least 4 of 5 notice Chinese sentence-to-star correspondence;
- at least 3 of 5 understand the story gist before needing a full English sentence translation;
- at least 3 of 5 intentionally auto-pause on a Chinese story word;
- at least 4 of 5 Resume and deliberately direct the next beat;
- at least 4 of 5 recall or recognize one explored Chinese word later in the session;
- at least 4 of 5 want to continue, replay, or propose the next event.

### Concern threshold

Revise and retest when:

- 2 of 5 require explicit instruction at the same stage;
- 2 or more repeat the same state or relationship misunderstanding;
- 3 or more navigate primarily by English while rarely attending to Chinese characters;
- 3 or more cannot understand story gist until the full English translation is shown;
- accidental story-word taps equal or exceed intentional taps;
- median time to first story exceeds 7 minutes because of confusion;
- themes or visuals are described as babyish by 2 or more learners;
- learners enjoy animation but show little attention to Chinese.

### Failure threshold

Do not expand scope; redesign the relevant core behavior when:

- 3 or more of 5 cannot complete Explore → Story → Pause/explore → Resume without step-by-step instruction;
- 3 or more treat relationship choice as a test with one correct route;
- 3 or more cannot identify new Current after movement;
- 3 or more cannot distinguish Related from Similar in the curated examples;
- 3 or more do not understand why the second region or story stars appeared;
- 3 or more experience auto-pause as an error or termination;
- Chinese difficulty prevents story participation even with designed scaffolds;
- the AI ignores anchors, produces linguistically invalid Chinese relationships, or repeatedly exceeds the intended proficiency;
- any learner encounters unsafe, humiliating, worldview-imposing, or privacy-compromising content.

## After each test

Separate raw observation from interpretation. Change no more than one or two related rules before the next learner unless safety, invalid Chinese, or a complete blocker demands immediate correction. Update [Prototype Decisions](prototype_decisions.md) with evidence, decision, and test date.
