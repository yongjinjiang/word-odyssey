# Word Odyssey: Story Generation Experience

## Authority of this document

This document is authoritative for story readiness, anchors, AI/child roles, beat structure, word lighting, pause-and-explore behavior, continuity, and story safety. View layout belongs to [Graph Interaction Model](graph_interaction_model.md); exact state definitions belong to [Node and Edge States](node_and_edge_states.md).

## Story premise

A Word Odyssey story grows from territory the child explored and ingredients the child chose. It is not generated from a blank prompt.

> The story lights up the forest.

As short beats appear, meaningful words illuminate across a darkened forest. Familiar words reactivate, useful new words appear, and every lit explorable word can become a new expedition.

## Co-creative roles

### The child directs

The child chooses explored regions, anchors, characters, avatars, animals, objects, places, feelings, surprises, branches, and when to pause or continue.

### The AI connects

The AI:

- uses confirmed anchors meaningfully;
- connects the child’s choices coherently;
- models level-appropriate language;
- adds restrained humor and surprise;
- preserves continuity;
- leaves room for the next child decision;
- does not resolve the whole adventure too quickly.

For Prototype 1, the AI composes primarily in Simplified Chinese appropriate to approximately Chinese elementary-school Grade 3 while respecting the interests and emotional maturity of a 12-year-old. English scaffolding explains meaning; it does not become the main narrative language.

## Story readiness and anchors

Story mode becomes available when there is enough material for one coherent event. Prototype 1 uses two explored regions plus two confirmed anchors; a preset-avatar character is optional but encouraged. This is a prototype default, not a long-term rule.

An anchor is a child-confirmed promise to the story. Before starting, the child can review, replace, or remove anchors. Every confirmed anchor must appear meaningfully in the first beat or be clearly set up for the next.

## Story beats

A beat is normally one or two short sentences. It should:

- advance one event or reveal;
- use or set up child-chosen material;
- introduce limited novelty;
- fit the chosen theme and reading level;
- create an opening for choice or exploration;
- avoid premature resolution.

Prototype 1 targets one or two new explorable words per beat and no more than four lit language nodes at once. Child testing may adjust those quantities.

## Which sentence words light up

Not every written token becomes a node. Prototype 1 lights a word or short phrase only if it is meaningful and useful to explore.

Selection priority:

1. confirmed anchor words when they appear;
2. a previously explored content word whose reuse supports recognition;
3. one or two new concrete, vivid, or action-bearing words central to the event;
4. a character node when that character acts or speaks.

Do not light:

- ordinary function words or particles such as routine uses of 的, 了, 在, or other grammatical glue;
- every repeated word in a sentence;
- incidental vocabulary that does not help explain or direct the beat;
- abstract or difficult words merely because they are novel.

Multiword expressions may light as one explorable phrase when splitting them would distort meaning.

Lit nodes use Chinese characters as their identity. Pinyin and a concise English gloss may appear on tap, first introduction, or explicit support request. Full English sentence translation should be available on demand rather than permanently competing with the Chinese story.

The default is validated if children can point from the sentence to the lit nodes, remember at least one reappearing word, and choose a new word intentionally. Reduce the count if their gaze scatters or they tap randomly; increase only if they consistently ask why important story words are not available.

## Spatial appearance of story words

Existing nodes reactivate in their stable locations. A new story word appears near a relevant region, along a Narrative bridge, or just beyond an active region according to the ordered rule in [Graph Interaction Model](graph_interaction_model.md). Its first edge is Narrative, never an invented language fact.

Character and identity nodes remain visually distinct from vocabulary nodes.

## Pause and explore

Prototype 1 supports both paths:

- tapping a lit explorable story word automatically pauses and enters Adventure View;
- an explicit Pause control freezes the beat without selecting a word.

On word tap:

1. narration and story motion stop immediately;
2. the tapped word becomes Current;
3. story state is preserved;
4. semantic relationship controls appear;
5. new exploration joins the child’s map;
6. Resume returns to the same narrative moment.

Auto-pause is validated if children discover it naturally and understand why the view changed. Require explicit Pause first only if accidental taps are common or children interpret the transition as the story ending.

## Director’s toolbox

Story View may offer small optional categories:

- 加入人物 Add a character
- 加入动物 Add an animal
- 加入物品 Add an object
- 加入地点 Add a place
- 加入感受 Add a feeling
- 加入难题 Add a problem
- 加入意外 Add a surprise
- 决定下一步 Choose what happens next

The toolbox should appear as optional direction, not a demand after every sentence. Prototype 1 uses nicknames and preset avatars. It does not request real names or personal photos.

## Continuity across exploration

The story preserves:

- setting and theme;
- character identities;
- recent events;
- current goal or problem;
- important objects;
- used and promised anchors;
- tone and reading level;
- unresolved choice;
- newly explored words eligible for later beats.

A short coherent chapter is preferable to an endless inconsistent story.

## Learning behavior

New Chinese story language should be supported by context, imagery, pinyin, concise English glosses, optional full-sentence translation, narration, reuse, or tap-to-explore. Story remains narrative first, not a constant quiz. English support should be easy to obtain but should not pre-translate every decision before the learner attempts to understand the Chinese.

## Safety, identity, and worldview

- Content remains age-appropriate and emotionally safe.
- Humor does not humiliate the child or a real person.
- Personal identity features use explicit parent-facing privacy choices.
- The story does not infer a worldview from identity or profile data.
- Future research may explore family-selectable values or faith-informed themes, but only through explicit family choice; they are never inferred or silently imposed.
- The AI does not present itself as a real friend, authority, or private confidant.

## Story completion

A chapter ends by child choice or at a natural pause. It acknowledges anchors and decisions, shows the Narrative route across the forest, preserves discovered words, and offers continuation without grading imagination.
